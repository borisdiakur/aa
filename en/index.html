Andrey Glazshneyder

Andrey Igorevich Glazshneyder, born on Oc­to­ber 26, 1966, in Budapest, is a promi­nent fi­gure in inter­natio­nal ballet and is lis­ted in the ency­clo­pedia of Russian ballet. He re­ceived his for­mative edu­cation at the Moscow State Aca­demy of Cho­reo­gra­phy from 1980 to 1986, under the gui­dance of A.I. Bon­da­ren­ko. After gra­du­ating, he joined the pre­sti­gious Sta­ni­slav­ski and Ne­mi­ro­vich-Dan­chen­ko Thea­ter in Mos­cow in 1986, where he danced both solo and lea­ding roles.

Glazshneyder par­ti­cu­lar­ly made a name for him­self as the first inter­pre­ter of the role of Mer­cu­tio in V.V. Va­siliev's pro­duc­tion in 1990. His re­per­toire in­cludes an im­pressive list of roles in classic ballet pieces such as Basil in "Don Qui­xote," Bir­banto in "Le Cor­saire," James in "La Syl­phide," Fi­ga­ro in "Bra­vo Fi­ga­ro," Al­bert in "Gi­sel­le," Sie­gfried in "Swan Lake," as the Prince in "The Nut­cracker" and "Cin­de­rel­la," Prince Dé­si­ré in "Sleep­ing Beauty," as well as the Jes­ter in "Swan Lake" and Bacchus in "Wal­pur­gis Night."

As a guest solo­ist, he per­for­med at the "Bal­let Moscow" theater and was ho­no­red as a laureate at in­ter­na­tio­nal com­pe­ti­tions.

Glaz­shney­der worked with some of the greatest names in ballet, in­clu­ding V.V. Va­si­liev, M.L. Ro­stro­povich, D.A. Bryan­tsev, L.I. Se­men­yako, An­na Pli­sets­kaya, Na­talya Le­dov­skaya, Mar­ga­ri­ta Dro­zdo­va, and Ga­li­na Kro­pi­vina. His ca­reer took him on tours through Ja­pan, the USA, South Ame­ri­ca, Europe, and China, where he de­lighted his audience with his art.

From 2000 to 2004, he was the lea­ding so­lo­ist at the Lviv Na­tio­nal Aca­de­mic Ope­ra and Bal­let Thea­ter Kru­she­l­nytska and si­mul­ta­neous­ly en­ga­ged in the edu­ca­tion of young ta­lents. He con­ti­nued his pe­da­go­gi­cal ca­reer from 2004 to 2008 at the Na­tio­nal Aca­de­my of Arts in Lviv and from 2008 to 2022 at the Kyiv Cho­reo­gra­phic Col­lege "Kiya­noch­ka," where he pas­sed on his ex­ten­sive ex­pe­rience and know­led­ge to the next ge­ne­ra­tion of bal­let dan­cers, pre­pa­ring them to en­ter hi­gher edu­ca­tion in various coun­tries around the world.

Anna Bielova

Anna Dmitrievna Bielova, born on No­vem­ber 10, 1991, in Uk­raine, com­ple­ted her bal­let edu­ca­tion at the pres­ti­gious cho­reo­gra­phic school "Kiya­noch­ka," where she stu­died from 2009 to 2013.

After her graduation, Bie­lo­va went on tour with the Na­tio­nal Opera of Uk­raine at their in­vi­ta­tion. Her skills and de­di­ca­tion led her in 2014 to the Kyiv Mo­dern Bal­let under the di­rec­tion of Radu Po­kli­ta­ru, where she stayed until 2016 and fur­ther de­ve­lo­ped her skills in con­tem­po­ra­ry ballet.

Her career then took her to the Bal­let En­sem­ble of the In­ter­na­tio­nal Bal­let Mos­cow, with which she toured from 2016 to 2018. This ex­pe­rience ex­pan­ded her re­per­toi­re and in­ter­na­tio­nal pre­sence.

From 2018 to 2020, Bielova was part of the St. Pe­ters­burg Bal­let, Bal­let on the Neva and Mos­cow Clas­si­cal Bal­let, where she honed her clas­si­cal bal­let skills.

From 2020 to 2021, she joined the In­ter­na­tio­nal Clas­si­cal Bal­let Kyiv and the Ma­gi­cal Bal­let in the USA. Her in­ter­na­tio­nal tours took Bie­lo­va through the USA, South Ame­ri­ca, Europe, Tai­wan, Ma­lay­sia, Austra­lia, and China.

In addition to her stage career, Bie­lova also de­di­cates herself to tea­ching. From 2020 to 2022, she was a tea­cher at the Dy­na­mo Ballet School in Kyiv, where she pas­sed on her know­ledge and ex­pe­rience to young dan­cers.

Her work as a cho­reo­gra­pher, es­pe­cially in pre­pa­ring child­ren and artists for in­ter­na­tio­nal com­pe­ti­tions, has nur­tured and in­spi­red nu­me­rous ta­lents.
