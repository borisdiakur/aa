Andrey Glazshneyder

Andrey Igorevich Glazshneyder, born on October 26, 1966, in Budapest, is a prominent figure in international ballet and is listed in the encyclopedia of Russian ballet. He received his formative education at the Moscow State Academy of Choreography from 1980 to 1986, under the guidance of A.I. Bondarenko. After graduating, he joined the prestigious Stanislavski and Nemirovich-Danchenko Theater in Moscow in 1986, where he danced both solo and leading roles.

Glazshneyder particularly made a name for himself as the first interpreter of the role of Mercutio in V.V. Vasiliev's production in 1990. His repertoire includes an impressive list of roles in classic ballet pieces such as Basil in "Don Quixote," Birbanto in "Le Corsaire," James in "La Sylphide," Figaro in "Bravo Figaro," Albert in "Giselle," Siegfried in "Swan Lake," as the Prince in "The Nutcracker" and "Cinderella," Prince Désiré in "Sleeping Beauty," as well as the Jester in "Swan Lake" and Bacchus in "Walpurgis Night."

As a guest soloist, he performed at the "Ballet Moscow" theater and was honored as a laureate at international competitions. Glazshneyder worked with some of the greatest names in ballet, including V.V. Vasiliev, M.L. Rostro­povich, D.A. Bryantsev, L.I. Semenyako, Anna Plisetskaya, Natalya Le­dov­skaya, Margarita Drozdova, and Galina Kropivina. His career took him on tours through Japan, the USA, South America, Europe, and China, where he delighted his audience with his art.

From 2000 to 2004, he was the leading soloist at the Lviv National Academic Opera and Ballet Theater Krushelnytska and simultaneously engaged in the education of young talents. He continued his pedagogical career from 2004 to 2008 at the National Academy of Arts in Lviv and from 2008 to 2022 at the Kyiv Choreographic College "Kiyanochka," where he passed on his extensive experience and knowledge to the next generation of ballet dancers, preparing them to enter higher education in various countries around the world.

Anna Bielova

Anna Dmitrievna Bielova, born on November 10, 1991, in Ukraine, completed her ballet education at the prestigious choreographic school "Kiyanochka," where she studied from 2009 to 2013.

After her graduation, Bielova went on tour with the National Opera of Ukraine at their invitation. Her skills and dedication led her in 2014 to the Kyiv Modern Ballet under the direction of Radu Poklitaru, where she stayed until 2016 and further developed her skills in contemporary ballet.

Her career then took her to the Ballet Ensemble of the International Ballet Moscow, with which she toured from 2016 to 2018. This experience expanded her repertoire and international presence.

From 2018 to 2020, Bielova was part of the St. Petersburg Ballet, Ballet on the Neva and Moscow Classical Ballet, where she honed her classical ballet skills.

From 2020 to 2021, she joined the International Classical Ballet Kyiv and the Magical Ballet in the USA. Her international tours took Bielova through the USA, South America, Europe, Taiwan, Malaysia, Australia, and China.

In addition to her stage career, Bielova also dedicates herself to teaching. From 2020 to 2022, she was a teacher at the Dynamo Ballet School in Kyiv, where she passed on her knowledge and experience to young dancers.

Her work as a choreographer, especially in preparing children and artists for international competitions, has nurtured and inspired numerous talents.
